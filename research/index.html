Computational Pathology

Main Contact: Xiao Han , Jing Zhang

Our team has been dedicated to advancing computational pathology through innovative artificial intelligence frameworks, aiming to address critical challenges in histopathological image analysis for cancer diagnosis and prognosis. Leveraging large-scale whole-slide image (WSI) datasets, we focus on developing generalizable, robust, and interpretable models that bridge the gap between engineering innovation and clinical needs. Our research spans across weakly supervised learning, self-supervised representation learning, multi-center validation, and task-specific segmentation/detection algorithms, with the mission to enhance diagnostic efficiency, reduce inter-observer variability, and facilitate precision medicine in pathology.

Our computational pathology core team focuses on:
  • Generalizable multi-modal feature learning: Developing frameworks to extract transferable features from whole-slide images, enabling cross-task applications in cancer detection, molecular profiling, and prognostic prediction by linking histological patterns to survival-relevant biomarkers.
  • Interpretable pathological image retrieval: Designing semantic-based methods to retrieve similar whole-slide images and highlight morphologically relevant regions, supporting clinical decision-making, medical research, and cohort analysis through interpretable case matching.
  • Integrated prognostic modeling: Combining histopathological features with clinical/genomic data to build predictive models for survival risk stratification, facilitating personalized treatment strategies and evidence-based precision oncology through histological-clinical outcome correlations.

ML in Biomedical Image Analysis

Main Contact: Xiao Han , Jing Zhang , Zhonglan Tang

Our team has focused on advancing machine learning for biomedical image analysis, addressing clinical needs across non-pathological medical imaging modalities. By integrating multi-modal data (e.g., CT scans, MRI, tongue images, behavioral videos) and developing adaptive algorithms, we aim to enhance diagnostic precision, treatment planning, and longitudinal health monitoring, with a focus on translating computational insights into actionable clinical solutions.

Our Biomedical Image Analysis team focuses on:
  • CT-based tumor characterization and prognostic modeling: Using deep learning to classify tumor phenotypes (e.g., lung, abdominal lesions) from CT scans and predict growth dynamics, enabling personalized treatment strategies and longitudinal disease progression monitoring.
  • Traditional medicine-inspired image analysis for TCM diagnostics: Developing automated frameworks to analyze tongue image features (color, texture, morphology) for objective assessment of Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM) health states, bridging qualitative diagnostic practices with data-driven precision.
  • Neuroimaging and behavioral analysis for mental health assessment: Applying machine learning to MRI/fMRI data for ADHD subtype classification and integrating video-based behavioral features to develop stress/mental health grading models, supporting early intervention and non-invasive diagnostic tools.
  • Image-guided interventional planning for minimally invasive procedures: Leveraging machine learning to optimize lung biopsy/ablation trajectories from CT/MRI data, balancing lesion accessibility and anatomical safety to reduce procedural risks and improve interventional outcomes.

AI in Medical Informatics

Main Contact: Ling He

Our team has focused on leveraging AI for medical informatics, specializing in the analysis and interpretation of one-dimensional medical signals to address unmet clinical needs in automated diagnosis and disease prediction. By developing adaptive signal processing frameworks and machine learning models, we aim to transform raw physiological signals into actionable insights, bridging the gap between computational analysis and precision patient care.

Our AI Medical Informatics team focuses on:
  • Automated mental disorder classification via near-infrared spectroscopy (NIRS): Analyzing cerebral hemodynamic signals from NIRS to distinguish multiple mental health conditions (e.g., depression, anxiety, ADHD), leveraging deep learning to identify subtle functional brain activity patterns and enable non-invasive, objective diagnostic support.
  • Bowel sound analysis for early disease detection: Developing signal processing pipelines to characterize bowel sound for automated prediction and classification of gastrointestinal disorders, particularly targeting early detection of neonatal necrotizing enterocolitis (NEC) through temporal-frequency feature extraction and predictive modeling.

Intelligent physiological monitoring and intervention Research

Main Contact: Gang Yang

Since 2018, we have been dedicated to developing wireless, integrated, and low-cost intelligent physiological monitoring devices, addressing critical clinical issues through innovative engineering methods. The series of devices developed by our team have been applied in multiple scenarios including neonatal intensive care, cardiovascular research, intraoperative neural protection, respiratory rehabilitation, and sleep health. Our core technologies cover the entire chain from sensor design, low-power circuits, multimodal algorithms to clinical validation. Our mission is to promote the popularization of precision medical devices, reduce medical costs through technology integration, and enhance the accessibility of diagnosis and treatment..

Our Intelligent physiological monitoring team focuses on:
  • Research and development of non-invasive integrated monitoring devices: Developing multi-parameter synchronous monitoring systems (such as jaundice, ECG, blood oxygen, body temperature, etc.), breaking through the limitations of traditional single-point monitoring, and applying them in neonatal intensive care and animal model research to achieve continuous dynamic assessment (such as monitoring the heart function of mice with myocardial infarction under anesthesia).
  • Innovation in clinical intervention devices: Designing real-time feedback medical devices (such as intraoperative foot monitors, respiratory trainers) to solve clinical pain points such as early warning of surgical nerve injury and improving compliance in pulmonary rehabilitation through hardware-software collaboration.
  • Breakthroughs in portable health management technology: Developing multi-parameter sleep monitoring devices that can replace the gold standard, integrating EEG, blood oxygen, and snoring analysis algorithms to promote the daily management of chronic diseases.
